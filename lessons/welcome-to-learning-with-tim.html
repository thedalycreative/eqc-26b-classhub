Welcome to learning with Tim

No jargon for the sake of it. No death-by-PowerPoint. Just real-world skills, taught by someone who still builds this stuff for a living — and genuinely wants you to get good at it.

The short version

I'm Tim — your trainer.

By day I'm in the room with you, teaching ICT and web development. The rest of the time I run a small creative studio building websites, brand work, and automation tools for real clients with real deadlines.

That matters for one reason: everything I teach you, I've actually shipped. This isn't theory I read once in a manual — it's the stuff I use on Monday for a paying client and explain to you on Tuesday.

Where I come from

I came up through deadlines, not just textbooks.

I run The Daly Creative, a Perth studio doing brand identity, web development, and AI automation. I learned this trade the hands-on way — client feedback, shipped projects, and the occasional 2am "why won't this deploy" moment.

I bring that straight into the classroom. We use the same tools the industry actually uses — modern web frameworks, real version control, proper deployment — taught the way I wish someone had taught me: clearly, practically, and without making you feel small for not knowing yet.

How I run the room

The vibe, in five rules.

01

Questions are the point

There's no such thing as a dumb question in here. The one you're too embarrassed to ask is usually the one five other people are also stuck on. Ask it.

02

Mistakes are data

Broken code isn't failure — it's information. Half this job is reading an error message and going "right, okay." We debug, we don't panic.

03

We build real things

You'll leave with actual projects you can show people — not worksheets. If it wouldn't fly in industry, we're not wasting your time on it.

04

Straight talk, always kind

I'll give you honest feedback because sugar-coating helps no one. But it'll always be about the work, never about you — and always with a way forward.

05

Keep it moving

I like a class that has momentum — short bursts, hands on keyboards, real examples. If you're bored, something's gone wrong and I want to know.

06

You're an adult here

I'll treat you like the professional you're training to become. Show up, give it a crack, and I'll back you the whole way.

The deal

Here's the two-way street.

🤝 What you get from me

  • Industry-current skills. I teach what's actually being used right now, not what was hot five years ago.
  • Real availability. Stuck? Ask. I'd rather you ask early than struggle silently for a week.
  • Clear expectations. You'll always know what we're doing, why it matters, and what "good" looks like.
  • Respect. For your time, your background, and the fact you're here to level up.

✅ What I ask of you

  • Show up and engage. You don't have to be the smartest in the room — just present and willing.
  • Have a crack. Try it before you ask. A wrong attempt teaches you more than a blank screen.
  • Be honest when you're lost. "I don't get it" is a complete sentence and a totally valid one.
  • Look out for each other. The best classes feel like a team, not twenty solo missions.
